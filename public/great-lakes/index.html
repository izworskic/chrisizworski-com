The Great Lakes

Live conditions, freighters, maritime history, and ecology from Bay City and Saginaw Bay

Chris Izworski is based in Bay City, Michigan, on Saginaw Bay, the largest embayment of Lake Huron, and one of the most productive freshwater bays in the world. The Great Lakes are the backdrop to everything here: the freighters that transit the bay each navigation season, the Soo Locks opening in late March, the ecology of a watershed that holds twenty percent of the world's surface fresh water, and the history of two centuries of commercial navigation on inland seas.

The content below is built from years of watching the lakes from Bay City, tracking vessels on AIS, reading water levels, fishing tributaries, and paying attention to what moves on and around the Great Lakes throughout the year. Start with the live tools for today's conditions, then continue into the guides, history, and ecology.
