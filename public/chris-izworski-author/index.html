Chris Izworski is a Michigan writer whose published work spans emergency communications, artificial intelligence, Great Lakes maritime, and Michigan trout fishing. His writing appears in trade publications, on his own sites, and as the byline on a set of daily publications he runs.

Trade publication

"The Unstoppable Wave of Artificial Intelligence"

The Call, Issue 51 (2025), pages 8 through 11. Published by NENA, The 9-1-1 Association. A four-page cover feature on the practical realities of deploying AI in emergency communications, written from firsthand operational experience. Still the clearest short-form statement of Chris's position on where AI actually belongs inside a 911 center, what it cannot do, and what operators should ask vendors before signing anything.

Daily publications

Michigan Trout Daily

daily.michigantroutreport.com publishes one stream-condition report every morning during the Michigan trout season. Each report covers a single river and includes live USGS gauge data, the current flow compared to the thirty-year historical median, water temperature, NWS weather forecast, sunrise and sunset times, matched hatches for the current water temperature, and a plain read on whether the river is actually fishable that day. The complete archive is at daily.michigantroutreport.com/chris-izworski.

Great Lakes Gazette

gazette.chrisizworski.com publishes a daily maritime brief covering Great Lakes shipping, port activity, and marine conditions. Each issue is assembled from real sources: AIS vessel tracking, NOAA water levels, NWS marine forecasts, and ice cover data in winter. The full archive is at gazette.chrisizworski.com/chris-izworski.

Ongoing essays

Freighter View Farms

freighterviewfarms.com collects Chris's longer-form writing on Zone 6a gardening, seed-saving, heritage vegetables, and the specific conditions of Michigan's Thumb region. The voice here is the most personal of the properties.
