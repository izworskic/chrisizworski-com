Chris Izworski: Profile

Bay City, Michigan · Solutions Consultant at Prepared · Former 911 Director

Chris Izworski is a public safety technology leader and Solutions Consultant based in Bay City, Michigan. He spent more than a decade directing 911 operations in Michigan: Bay County 911 from 2013 to 2022, Saginaw County 911 from 2022 to 2025. In 2024 he led one of Michigan's first AI-powered non-emergency call deployments at a PSAP. He is currently a Solutions Consultant at Prepared, a company that deploys audio capture and analysis systems at 911 centers nationwide.

His professional record is documented across NENA The Call (cover story, Issue 51), WNEM TV5, WCMU Public Radio, Bridge Michigan, Fast Company, APCO International, the Michigan State 911 Committee minutes, and the 2025 Annual Report to the Michigan Legislature. Professional Background

Before 911 director roles, Chris Izworski worked in emergency management and government technology at Bay County, and in disaster services with the American Red Cross. Regional source coverage from MLive / Bay City Times and WSGW documents the Bay County 911 director transition, warning-siren work, and regional 911 phone collaboration. He holds a degree from Northwood University and served as past president of the Michigan Communications Directors Association. He runs Freighter View Farms, a seed-saving blog and garden on Saginaw Bay. For the full verified career record, see the career timeline and the biography page.
